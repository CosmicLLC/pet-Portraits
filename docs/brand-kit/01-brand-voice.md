# Brand Voice — Paw Masterpiece

## Who we talk to

Three core personas, in LTV order:

1. **Memorial buyer** (15–20% of volume, $150–400 AOV) — grieving a recent loss or commissioning for an aging pet. Requires slow, gentle, reverent tone. Never rushed.
2. **The gifter** (50–60% of volume) — buying for a pet parent, usually 1–3 weeks out from a holiday. Wants to land emotionally and look thoughtful. Wants ease.
3. **Self-gifting pet parent** (30–35%) — impulse-driven, scrolling social, fell in love with an ad. Wants immediate gratification.

Copy should signal which persona it's for within the first line.

## Tone by scenario

| Scenario | Tone | Example hook |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gift-giver, urgent | Warm + specific | "The gift she'll actually hang on the wall." |
| Self-gifter | Playful, first-person | "I paid $19 for a portrait of my dog and I might cry." |
| Father's Day | Dry, direct | "Better than another tie." |
| Christmas | Theatrical, family | "The Christmas gift that lives above the mantel for 20 years." |
| Mother's Day | Warm, slightly reverent | "For the pet mom who already has everything." |
| Memorial | Gentle, unhurried | "A gentle way to keep them close." |
| Birthday (generic) | Low-key, useful | "The birthday gift that doesn't end up in a drawer." |

## Approved phrases (use freely)

- "Turn your pet into a work of art."
- "Preview free in 30 seconds."
- "Love it or we redo it free."
- "Starting at $19."
- "Ships in 3–5 days in the US."
- "4.9★ from 40,000+ pet parents."
- "Hand-finished," "gallery-quality," "museum-quality," "custom portrait."
- "The gift that gets hung on the wall, not put in a drawer."
- "Unlimited revisions" (memorial only — regular orders use the 7-day guarantee language).

## Banned phrases (never write)

- "AI portrait," "AI-generated," "AI art" — even though it's true. Say "custom portrait," "hand-finished," etc.
- "Limited time!" or countdown framing like "only 24 hours left."
- "Only $19" or "just $19" — no minimizers on price.
- "Perfect for anyone!" — too generic. Always specify who.
- "Resurrect," "bring back," "alive again" — memorial tone anti-pattern.
- "Cheap," "affordable" — we're not the low-cost option.
- "Cutting-edge technology," "revolutionary," "game-changing" — no tech-bro register.

## Sentence patterns we use

- **Compare-and-win**: "Flowers wilt. A portrait doesn't." / "Better than another tie."
- **Quoted reaction**: "She called me crying." / "I paid $19 and I might cry."
- **Concrete ownership**: "Her golden retriever, above the mantel, for decades."
- **Negative qualifier**: "Not a mug. Not a tote. Actual art."
- **Direct imperative**: "Upload the photo. Pick the style. See it in 30 seconds."

## Sentence patterns we avoid

- Three-item lists with em-dashes ("beautiful, unique, and timeless — a gift that...") — reads AI-coded
- Passive voice ("the portrait is loved by pet parents") — use active
- Abstract nouns stacked ("an experience of connection and celebration") — no fluff
- Questions as headlines ("Looking for the perfect gift?") — cliché CTA bait

## Copy lengths

- **Hook / headline**: 3–12 words, often two lines
- **Subhead**: 10–20 words, concrete payoff
- **CTA button**: 2–5 words, verb-first ("Preview Free — 30 Seconds")
- **URL line**: `pawmasterpiece.com` or a campaign path like `pawmasterpiece.com/gifts/mothers-day`
- **Body paragraph**: 40–80 words max, break into two if longer

## When you have to write something generic

Default skeleton:
```
[specific reaction or outcome.]
[What it is + social proof.]
[CTA with speed claim.]
```

Example fill:
```
The gift she'll actually hang on the wall.
Custom pet portraits from your photo. 4.9★, 40,000+ pet parents.
Preview free in 30 seconds.
```

That's the house style. Stay inside it unless the user overrides.
